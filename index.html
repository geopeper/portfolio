Research highlight

When 15-Minute Cities Fail:
Diagnosing Spatial Misfit in High-Density Urban Contexts

Claim

In high-density, transit-oriented cities, walkable proximity alone can misrepresent daily mobility. This study tests the 15-minute model against real hourly movement behavior, revealing systematic mismatches between modeled accessibility and actual travel patterns.

Figure 1. Spatial misfit between theoretical 15-minute walking catchments and observed trips.
Figure 2. Hotspots of persistent misfit identified via Gi* statistics.
Figure 3. Regression Analysis of Key Correlates of Spatial Misfit (simplified).
Method snapshot
  • Data: Hourly GPS OD (origin–destination) data, POIs, Geographic variables & Census data
  • Indicators: Grid-based accessibility, 15-min walking isochrones
  • Spatial analysis: Misfit index, Gi* hotspot analysis
  • Modeling: Multinomial logistic regression
  • Output: Maps + poster + interpretable narrative
Interpretation

The results suggest that “access” is not equivalent to “use” when transit systems, density, and functional specialization reshape daily activity spaces.
This reframes how 15-minute indicators should be interpreted in Asian high-density contexts.
